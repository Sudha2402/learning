"A sibling may be the keeper of one's identity, the only person with the keys to one's unfettered, more fundamental self." 

Raksha Bandhan is the celebration of this sacred bond, created by the all-knowing creator! It seems like destiny put you'll together like it obviously knew what you'd turn out to be ever before your birth. Your brother is your first friend ever, your confidante, your guide, your philosopher, your escape plan maker, your alibi during late night outs, your shoulder when you need one to cry on and your protector for life. He plays so many roles, and all at once, and all with such ease and elan, it only gets you to admire him even more. No wonder our ancestors had a designated day to simply celebrate this beautiful bond and had symbolic ways to strengthen it even further! Raksha Bandhan literally translates to "knot of protection:. The history of the festival dates back to over 5000 years when Draupadi of the famed Mahabharat tied it around Lord Krishna's wrist. And since then, for eons, sisters have tied a protective thread in after a ceremonial arti where the brother is offered auspicious items like sweets, vermillion, FLOWERS and dry fruits. There are several stories relating to the origin of the festival, but whatever it maybe, it sure helps sisters and brothers bond.
